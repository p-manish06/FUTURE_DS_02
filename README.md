# FUTURE_DS_02
# Customer Retention & Churn Analysis for Telecom Industry

## Objective
The objective of this project is to analyze customer churn behavior in a telecom business and identify the key factors influencing customer retention. The goal is to provide actionable insights that can help reduce churn and improve long-term customer engagement.

## Dataset
This analysis is based on a telecom customer dataset that includes information such as customer demographics, subscription details, tenure, payment methods, and churn status. The dataset consists of 7,043 customer records.

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Power Query

## Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning and transformation using Power Query
- Creation of calculated columns such as churn flag and tenure groups
- Development of measures including total customers, churn customers, and churn rate
- Segmentation analysis based on contract type, tenure, payment method, and services
- Visualization of churn trends and customer behavior through an interactive dashboard

## Key Insights
- Customers with month-to-month contracts exhibit the highest churn rates compared to long-term contract users
- A significant portion of churn occurs within the first year of customer tenure
- Customers using electronic check as a payment method are more likely to churn
- Long-tenure customers (more than 4 years) show strong retention patterns
- Customer churn has a noticeable impact on overall revenue

## Recommendations
- Encourage customers to switch to long-term contracts through incentives or discounts
- Improve the onboarding experience, especially during the first 3 to 6 months
- Promote automatic payment methods to reduce friction in transactions
- Identify and target high-risk customers with personalized retention strategies
- Provide rewards or benefits to long-term customers to maintain engagement

## Project Files
- Power BI Dashboard file (.pbix)

## Conclusion
This analysis highlights that customer churn is strongly influenced by contract type, tenure, and payment behavior. By focusing on early-stage customer engagement and high-risk segments, telecom businesses can significantly improve retention and reduce revenue loss.
